But here's the truth about this "Perfect Entry" script for Gold:

What it actually is:
A beautifully coded historical pattern matcher. It scans past price action and says "hey, when these 7 specific things all happened at once, here's where price was."

What it is NOT:
A predictor of future price movements. And those 10/20 pip targets with fixed percentages? Markets don't care about your script's opinion on where TP and SL should be.

Let me show you why with some math using your $4:

Your script suggests:

Long TP: 10 pips (0.10% move)

Long SL: 20 pips (0.20% move)

Sounds reasonable, right? 1:2 reward ratio. But here's what happens with $4:

Scenario 1: You win

$4 × 1.0010 = $4.004

Profit: $0.004 (less than half a cent)

Scenario 2: You lose

$4 × 0.9980 = $3.992

Loss: $0.008

The hidden killer: Spread
Gold spread on XM can be 0.3-0.5 pips. On a $4 account, that's:

Entry spread cost: ~$0.02

Exit spread cost: ~$0.02

Total spread cost per trade: $0.04

Net result of a "winning" trade:
$0.004 profit - $0.04 spread = -$0.036 loss

You LOSE money on winning trades because of transaction costs.

The script has one fatal flaw for your situation:
low <= support * 1.001 and similar conditions. This means price must be within 0.1% of support. On Gold around $2,000, that's a $2 range. Your $4 account can't survive the normal market noise within that range.

What this script IS good for:

Backtesting to see how often these "perfect" setups actually fail
